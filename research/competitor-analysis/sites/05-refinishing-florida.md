# Competitor Design/UX Research: refinishing-florida.com ("All Surface Refinishing")

**Pages analyzed (all verified canonical to refinishing-florida.com):** Homepage, /services/bathtub/, /tub/ (Gallery), /bathroom/ (Contact), /kitchen/ (Testimonials), /commercial/resurfacing/, /refinishing-services-in-fort-lauderdale-fl/, /bath-room/ (5-Year Guarantee), /blog/. Platform: WordPress + Divi 4.27 theme, JotForm embed, Google Site Kit, SearchAtlas SEO tooling.

## 1. Homepage structure (section-by-section order)
1. Top utility bar: "FOR A FREE ESTIMATE PLEASE CALL (772) 200-6619"
2. Header/nav (logo + menu) with second phone block: "FOR A FREE ESTIMATE PLEASE CALL (954) 271-3449"
3. Hero: H1 brand statement + "MORE THAN 14,000 REFINISHING PROJECTS COMPLETED" + "Specializing in:" service ticker + county list
4. Trust-icon banner row: SAVE thousands / TOP NOTCH quality + 5-yr warranty / 5-star Google reviews / WORLD CLASS service / FULLY LICENSED (icon badges)
5. Intro copy block ("Comprehensive Refinishing...") featuring "Frank's 30+ years"
6. Bulleted capability list ("We Can Refinish, Reglaze, Repaint, and Resurface:" — 14 items) + Angie's List callout
7. "Transform Instead of Replace" narrative section
8. Services section: 9 service blurbs, each with a "Click Here to Read More..." button
9. "Residential and Commercial Capabilities" section
10. "Why Choose Us" — 5 sub-blocks (Experience, Cost Savings, Fast/Clean, Safety, Satisfaction)
11. Gallery teaser (4 before/after images + "View Our Gallery" button)
12. Testimonials teaser (links to page, Google reviews plug)
13. Warranty and Quality Assurance section
14. Service Areas section (9 counties, ~90 cities in prose)
15. Massive on-page FAQ section (3 categories, ~27 Q&As with pricing)
16. "Get Started Today" closing CTA (call-focused)
17. Footer: keyword links (BATHTUB REGLAZING, etc.), license #, Wisetack financing disclaimer, agency credit, and a giant A–Z city/county link list

## 2. Hero headline
Paraphrase: "All Surface Refinishing — Florida's trusted experts in bathtub, countertop, and cabinet refinishing, resurfacing and painting," immediately followed by "more than 14,000 projects completed." Angle: **authority-by-volume and keyword saturation** — it's written to rank (stuffs refinish/resurface/reglaze/repaint variants) rather than to sell a transformation or feeling. No emotional or aspirational angle, no imagery-led promise.

## 3. Primary CTA
**"FOR A FREE ESTIMATE PLEASE CALL (772) 200-6619 / (954) 271-3449"** — a phone number, not a button action. Appears in the top utility bar, again beside the logo, as the first styled button on the page, repeated mid-page and in the closing "Get Started Today" section and footer. The entire conversion model is call-first.

## 4. Secondary CTA
"View Our Gallery" button and nine "Click Here to Read More about [Service]" buttons. On the commercial page: "Schedule Your Free Estimate" (links to the contact form). Recurring inline CTA: "Check out our 5-star reviews on Google" (appears 5+ times). No quote form, chat, or scheduler is offered above the fold anywhere.

## 5. Navigation items
Home | About Us | FAQ's | Services (9-item dropdown) | Commercial Services (dropdown: HOA & Property Managers, Commercial Bathtub Refinishing, Hotels) | Blog | 5 Year Guarantee | Gallery | Testimonials | Contact. Notably, nav slugs are mismatched legacy URLs: About Us = `/bath-tub/`, 5 Year Guarantee = `/bath-room/`, Gallery = `/tub/`, Testimonials = `/kitchen/`, Contact = `/bathroom/`.

## 6. Service categories
Nine residential services: Anti-Slip Bathtub & Shower Treatments; Bathroom Floor Refinishing; Bathtub Refinishing; Cabinet Refinishing; Countertop Refinishing; Cultured Marble Refinishing; Shower Refinishing; Tile/Tile Surround & Backsplash Refinishing; Vanity Refinishing. Plus a commercial tier (general resurfacing, HOA/property managers, hotels) and long-tail extras (hot tubs, jacuzzis, garage floors, desks, bathroom stalls — "virtually any surface").

## 7. Quote / booking flow
Two paths: (a) call one of two phone numbers; (b) find "Contact" in nav → `/bathroom/` page → fill a JotForm-embedded callback form → "a Specialist will call you back." **Friction points:** no online pricing/instant quote, no scheduling or calendar, no photo upload despite testimonials mentioning customers emailing photos for quotes, form promises a callback rather than a confirmed appointment, form lives only on the contact page (no inline homepage form), and CAPTCHA adds a step. Two different phone numbers (plus a third, (954) 787-3537, in a service-page footer) create confusion about which to call.

## 8. Form fields
JotForm embed: First Name*, Last Name*, Email*, Phone Number* (formatted (000) 000-0000), Comments ("Please tell us a little about what services you are looking for")*, and a "verify you are human" CAPTCHA. That's it — no service dropdown, no city/zip, no photo upload, no preferred time.

## 9. Trust signals
- "More than 14,000 refinishing projects completed"
- "Over 30 years" in business ("Frank's 30+ years"); location page says "over 20 years" (inconsistent)
- **Kohler Authorized Service Provider** badge (differentiator)
- Angie's List membership + BBB of Palm Beach County ("20 year history")
- "5-star reviews on Google" (repeated constantly, deep-linked)
- "Fully Licensed" icon + real license number in footer (CGC 016486)
- Wisetack financing availability
- Icon badge row: piggy bank (savings), 5 stars, world-class service, licensed

## 10. Warranty / guarantee language
A dedicated nav item and page: **5-Year Warranty on residential workmanship and materials** — explicitly covering paint peeling/lifting and epoxy delamination. Commercial/rental properties get reduced terms: 1 year (paint) / 2 years (epoxy). Homepage frames it as "Five-Year Warranty on workmanship and materials... this guarantee reflects our confidence." The guarantee page itself is skeletal — roughly six lines of text, no visual treatment, and even a typo on the homepage banner ("5 YEAR WARRANY").

## 11. Before-after gallery pattern
A single long gallery page organized into ~11 labeled category sections (Floors, Tub & Toilet, Tub Repair, Hot Tub, Tile & Tub, Bathroom Floor Tile, Kitchen Cabinet, Bathroom Cabinet & Vanity, Countertop, Home & Office Cabinets, Commercial). ~160 thumbnail images in grids with click-to-enlarge lightbox (Magnific Popup). **No before/after sliders, no side-by-side pairing, no captions or project stories** — many filenames are raw iPhone exports (UUID.jpeg) and date-stamps. Every image does have a descriptive (SEO-written) alt tag. Homepage teases 4 static "before-after" composite JPGs.

## 12. Review / testimonial strategy
Three layers: (1) constant inline "check out our 5-star reviews on Google" links pushing users off-site to the Google profile; (2) a dedicated Testimonials page with ~9 hand-pasted text quotes attributed by first name + city (Robyn, Boynton Beach; Amy, Boca Raton...) — no stars, photos, dates, or third-party verification widget; (3) Angie's List/BBB name-drops. No embedded review widget (Trustindex/Birdeye), no schema review markup, no video testimonials, no review snippets placed next to CTAs.

## 13. Color palette impression
Utilitarian and dated: a single teal/ocean-blue accent (#0581ab, #0880b3) over white, with near-black (#011a23) and light grays (#eceef1, #e1e5eb). Yellow 5-star graphics and multicolor clip-art icons in the trust banner. No cohesive premium palette — reads like a default Divi build with one brand color, closer to "local contractor" than "premium restoration."

## 14. Typography impression
Open Sans everywhere (weights 300–800) — the stock Divi/Google-font choice. Heavy use of ALL-CAPS banner lines with quotation marks ("SAVE", "TOP NOTCH", "WORLD CLASS") which reads as shouty 2010s-era local-services design. Long unbroken paragraph walls on service/location pages. No display face, no typographic hierarchy signaling premium craft.

## 15. Photography style
Mixed and inconsistent: (a) polished, stock-looking lifestyle interiors (modern bathrooms, epoxy floors, sunlit living rooms) used decoratively; (b) authentic but unedited technician job photos in the gallery (uneven lighting, raw phone uploads); (c) clip-art icon badges and animated GIF logos (logo is a .gif with a spray gun). Real-work authenticity is there, but zero art direction — no consistent crop, color grade, or before/after framing.

## 16. Mobile UX observations (inferred from markup)
Divi responsive theme with a standard hamburger `et_mobile_menu`; header is **not** fixed/sticky (no `et_fixed_nav`), so the phone CTA scrolls away — no persistent sticky call bar detected. Phone numbers are proper `tel:` links (click-to-call works). The extremely long homepage (hero → 27 FAQs → ~90-city footer) means brutal scroll depth on mobile; the giant footer city list and dual phone numbers add noise. Forms are on a separate page, adding taps. Carousel scripts (Slick) plus 160+ gallery images suggest heavy mobile page weight.

## 17. Commercial vs residential positioning
Genuinely dual-track, weighted residential-first. Commercial gets its own nav dropdown and dedicated pages targeting HOAs/property managers, hotels/motels, apartments, nursing homes, offices — with a distinct value prop (80% savings, minimal downtime, easier housekeeping, multi-unit programs, maintenance plans). But the commercial warranty is quietly much weaker (1–2 yrs vs 5), and commercial pages reuse the same call-only conversion path with no RFP/spec-sheet, case studies, or account-manager framing.

## 18. SEO page structure
- **URL patterns:** `/services/{short-slug}/` (bathtub, cabinet, tile...), `/commercial/{segment}/`, plus legacy keyword slugs (`/reglaze-tub/`, `/tub-repair/`, `/restore-tub/`, `/resurfacing/`, `/counter/`) and the mismatched nav slugs (`/tub/` = gallery).
- **Location pages:** two overlapping generations — `/refinishing-resurfacing-{city}/` (Hollywood, Miramar, Weston, Boca Raton, Jupiter, Wellington, Miami, Stuart, Miami Beach) and `/refinishing-services-in-{city}-fl/` (Fort Lauderdale, Pembroke Pines, West Palm Beach, Delray Beach, Pompano Beach, Palm Beach Gardens...), including a duplicate `/refinishing-services-in-fort-lauderdale-fl-2/`. Location pages are long, templated, keyword-repetitive ("residential vanity refinishing services in Fort Lauderdale, FL" as an H3 pattern), with some sloppiness (copy-paste typos like "t All Surface Refinishing," flooring copy pasted into a refinishing page).
- **Titles:** `{Service} in South Florida | All Surface Refinishing`; homepage title omits the brand. Title/content mismatches on legacy slugs (Gallery page titled "Bathtub Refinishing in Miami, FL").
- **H1s:** single keyword-dense H1 per page; homepage H1 stuffs four service keywords.
- **Schema:** LocalBusiness + PostalAddress, WebSite/Organization, SiteNavigationElement, ImageObject — but no Review, FAQPage, or Service schema despite huge FAQ content.
- Footer contains a ~150-item city/county link list (mostly unlinked text) — classic doorway-style geo footprint.

## 19. Content strategy
Aggressive and recent: a blog ("Bath Tub Refinishing Tips & Guides") publishing at a near-daily cadence (posts dated continuously through the current week), with cost-guide and "find a pro in {city}" style titles clearly produced via an SEO-content platform (SearchAtlas tooling is loaded on the site; footer credits an SEO agency, BullsEye Internet Marketing). Tag taxonomy (refinishing, reglazing, cabinet, tips). Massive on-page FAQ farm on the homepage with real price anchors ($350–$650 tubs; $3,000–$6,000 cabinets; $90–$150/door; $600–$1,000 tile) plus a separate FAQ page. No downloadable guides, videos, case studies, or email capture.

## 20. Weaknesses Lustre can exploit
1. **No online quote/booking:** call-only conversion with a buried callback form; a photo-upload instant-estimate or calendar-booked assessment flow would leapfrog them.
2. **Dated, non-premium design:** stock Divi + Open Sans + clip-art icons + shouty caps; a refined visual identity instantly signals a higher tier.
3. **Before/after presentation is wasted:** raw uncaptioned phone photos, no sliders, no project stories — premium galleries with interactive comparisons and captions would dominate.
4. **Trust inconsistencies:** conflicting "30+ yrs" vs "20 yrs," three different phone numbers, two brand names in footers ("All Surface Refinishing" vs "Frank's All Surface Refinishing"), typo in the warranty banner ("WARRANY").
5. **Weak commercial warranty (1–2 yrs) quietly contradicts the "5 Year Guarantee" nav promise** — a uniform strong guarantee is a direct attack line.
6. **Reviews outsourced to Google:** no on-site verified review widget, no review schema, anonymous first-name testimonials.
7. **Confusing information architecture:** nav slugs like /tub/ for Gallery and /bathroom/ for Contact, duplicate location pages, title/content mismatches — both a UX and crawl-quality liability.
8. **No sticky mobile CTA** despite a phone-first model; a persistent "Get a Quote / Call" bar wins mobile.
9. **Templated AI-ish content at scale** with visible copy-paste errors — beatable with fewer, genuinely authoritative local pages.
10. **They publish price anchors** ($350–$650 tubs, etc.) — Lustre can position above the price-shopper frame with a premium value narrative, or use transparent tiered pricing done better.

---

**Overall verdict:** This competitor is an SEO-strong but experience-weak presence — decades of domain history, relentless keyword coverage, near-daily content production, and legitimate trust assets (Kohler authorization, license, 14,000-project claim) likely earn them meaningful organic traffic across South Florida. However, the site converts almost entirely by phone call, looks like a dated local-contractor template, presents its best proof (real before/after work) carelessly, and is riddled with inconsistencies that undercut a premium impression. Lustre can't out-age their domain overnight, but it can decisively outclass them on design, conversion flow, proof presentation, and brand coherence — the exact dimensions that win the premium end of this market.
