# Competitor Research: AWS Wrap (awswrap.com)
**Prepared for Lustre Surface Restoration — pages crawled: /services/kitchen-vinyl-wrap, / (homepage), /services, /portfolio, /contact-us, /about-us, /pricing. No fetch failures.**

## 1. Homepage structure (section-by-section order)
1. Header/nav (sticky, with services dropdown + "Get a Free Quote" button)
2. Hero (vehicle-focused headline + dual CTAs)
3. CTA banner
4. Service overview
5. Interactive pricing calculator (van/vehicle configurator)
6. "Why Choose" benefits section
7. Services grid (9 automotive service cards)
8. Portfolio gallery (6 images, link to full portfolio)
9. Transformation CTA block
10. Testimonials (16 reviews)
11. Pricing plans (3 tiers)
12. Blog teaser section
13. FAQ accordion (8 questions)
14. Final CTA
15. Large multi-column footer (services, blog links, contact, social)

## 2. Hero headline
Paraphrase: "Turn heads, boost your brand, and protect your ride with premium wraps and protective films." The angle is a triple-benefit stack — aesthetics + business branding + protection — aimed squarely at **vehicles**, not surfaces. Kitchen/surface work gets zero hero presence; it's a bolt-on vertical. The kitchen page's own hero angle is "affordable, fast kitchen transformation without renovation" (cost-and-speed framing, not premium/craftsmanship framing).

## 3. Primary CTA
**"Get a Free Quote"** — appears in the top-right of the sticky nav, in the hero, and repeated mid-page and at page ends. On the kitchen page it's the first hero button (with arrow icon). It routes to /contact-us (a generic contact form, not a dedicated quote flow).

## 4. Secondary CTA
**"Our Portfolio" / "View Our Portfolio"** (hero, paired with the quote button). Tertiary CTAs: "Contact Us," "Learn More About Us," "View All Services," per-card "Learn More" arrows, plus click-to-call phone numbers and a floating WhatsApp button.

## 5. Navigation items
Home · Services (dropdown, 9 automotive submenu items) · Portfolio · Pricing · About Us · Blog · Contact Us · Gift Cards · Get a Free Quote (button). Notably, the nav dropdown surfaces only automotive services — kitchen/cabinet/furniture wraps are buried in the footer and the /services hub.

## 6. Service categories
The /services hub groups into three tiers:
- **Automotive Wraps** (8): PPF, vinyl color change, commercial wraps, custom car design, window tint, ceramic coating, Tesla wrap, Cybertruck wrap
- **Customization** (3): car polishing, painting calipers/mufflers, starlight headliner
- **Home & Specialty Wraps** (4): kitchen vinyl wrap, cabinet wrap, furniture wrap, boat wrap

Kitchen page sub-categories: solid color, textured/patterned (wood grain, marble, metallic), custom graphics, protective clear wraps.

## 7. Quote / booking flow
Single-step: every "Get a Free Quote" CTA dumps into the generic /contact-us form. There is no booking calendar, no service pre-selection, no multi-step wizard, no photo upload, no instant estimate for kitchens (the interactive calculator on the homepage is vehicle-only). Friction points: (a) users must describe their project in a free-text message box; (b) no required-field indicators; (c) no scheduling — promise is only "we'll reply within 24 hours"; (d) kitchen leads and car leads funnel into the same undifferentiated form.

## 8. Form fields
Contact/quote form: **Name, Email, Phone, Message** (+ hidden "Current URL" tracking field). Submit button: "Send Message," with Terms/Privacy acknowledgment. A newsletter signup and an expandable Name/Email/Phone mini-form also appear on service pages. No file upload, no service dropdown, no address/zip field, no budget selector.

## 9. Trust signals
- "Over 10 years of experience" (footer + about page)
- "Trusted by hundreds of customers"
- "Certified installers" (unspecified certifier)
- Material brand name-drops: 3M, Avery Dennison, Orafol
- 16 testimonials with 5-star indicators and Miami-area location tags
- Product claims: "blocks up to 99% of UV rays," "up to 10-year durability"
- First-responder discounts, low-VOC/eco claims
- Notably absent: review counts tied to a platform, BBB, actual certifications/awards, team photos, project counts.

## 10. Warranty / guarantee language
Kitchen page: "Warranty Included: 3–5 years on materials and installation." Elsewhere: up to 10 years on PPF, 5–7 years wrap durability, 2–5 years ceramic coating. The promise is duration-based and vague — no written warranty document, no "we'll fix it free" plain-language pledge, no satisfaction guarantee.

## 11. Before-after gallery pattern
Static thumbnail **grid** (14 images on /portfolio), no lightbox evident, **no before/after pairs, no captions, no project descriptions, no category filtering**. Worse: the gallery on the kitchen page is mostly **vehicle photos**, not kitchens — a direct relevance mismatch for a homeowner evaluating cabinet wraps.

## 12. Review / testimonial strategy
Quote-block format: text + customer first name/initial + neighborhood tag (North Miami, Hialeah, Kendall, etc.) + star rating. Placed as a dedicated mid-homepage section; implied Google-sourced but not embedded/verifiable (no widget, no review count, no links to individual reviews). A "write a review on Google" link exists in the footer. Reviews cover a scattered mix (yacht, furniture, headliner, kitchen cabinets), reinforcing generalist positioning.

## 13. Color palette impression
Dark, automotive-shop aesthetic: deep navy/charcoal backgrounds, white text, **bright red accent** on CTAs/arrows/logo, neutral grays. Reads aggressive and masculine — well-suited to car culture, poorly suited to a homeowner researching kitchen refinishing (feels like a tuning garage, not a home-improvement brand).

## 14. Typography impression
Bold modern sans-serif headlines with strong weight hierarchy; clean, high-contrast body copy; icon+text feature lists. Competent but generic — reads like a purchased template (footer credits an outside designer, Iconicline), with no distinctive brand voice in the type system.

## 15. Photography style
Polished, high-contrast professional **vehicle** photography (Teslas, luxury cars, close-up finish shots), consistent grading, Miami-flavored. Kitchen/surface photography is thin — one hero image on the kitchen page, and the supporting gallery recycles car images. No process shots, no team-at-work shots, no real-home environmental photography.

## 16. Mobile UX observations
- Floating WhatsApp chat button (bottom-right) — strong for their demographic
- Both phone numbers are click-to-call links
- Scroll-to-top affordance
- Sticky header with quote button persists
- Long single-column pages with accordion FAQs (mobile-friendly pattern)
- No evident sticky bottom CTA bar, no SMS option, no mobile booking widget. The 5x3 kitchen pricing table and comparison table will likely require horizontal scrolling on phones.

## 17. Commercial vs residential positioning
Primarily B2C automotive with a B2B fleet/commercial-wrap arm ("turn your fleet into mobile billboards"). The kitchen page explicitly segments four audiences: homeowners, renters, business owners, real-estate investors — a smart breadth play, but it dilutes premium positioning (renter-friendly "removable/temporary" framing is the opposite of a lasting-restoration promise). Home services are clearly a secondary expansion vertical, not core identity.

## 18. SEO page structure
- URL patterns: flat and clean — `/services/{service-slug}`, `/blog/{keyword-slug}`
- Title pattern: `{Service} + {City} | {Benefit} | AWS Wrap` (e.g., "Kitchen Vinyl Wrap Miami | Transform Your Kitchen Affordably | AWS Wrap")
- H1 pattern: keyword + city + benefit ("Kitchen Vinyl Wrap in Miami: Transform Your Kitchen with Style & Durability")
- Geo-targeting is split/confused: titles alternate between "Miami" and "Hollywood, FL"
- **No dedicated location/city landing pages** — geo-SEO rides entirely on keyword-stuffed titles and blog posts
- Heavy internal footer linking; FAQ accordions likely targeting FAQ schema

## 19. Content strategy
Active blog (15+ posts) — almost entirely automotive, formulaic local-SEO titles ("How Much PPF Costs in Miami…", "Tesla Wraps That Turn Heads…"). Zero kitchen/cabinet/surface content in the blog. FAQs appear on the homepage (8 Qs) and kitchen page (6 Qs, accordion: lifespan, removability, damage, surfaces, pricing, DIY vs pro). No downloadable guides, no cost calculators for home services, no care-guide content hub, no video.

## 20. Weaknesses Lustre can exploit
1. **Kitchen/surface work is an afterthought** — automotive brand, automotive nav, automotive blog. A dedicated surface-restoration specialist instantly out-positions them on focus and credibility.
2. **Gallery mismatch**: kitchen page shows car photos. Lustre should lead with abundant, captioned, real kitchen/surface before-afters (ideally slider comparisons — AWS has none).
3. **No true before/after presentation anywhere** — a slider-based B/A gallery with project stories is an open lane.
4. **Generic 4-field contact form, no booking** — a guided multi-step quote flow (service type → photos upload → zip → preferred date) or online scheduling would beat their 24-hour email promise.
5. **Weak, vague warranty** ("3–5 years") — a named, written, plain-English guarantee is an easy differentiator.
6. **Unverifiable testimonials** — embedded live Google review widget with count/rating would out-trust their static quote blocks.
7. **Budget/temporary framing** ("affordable," "removable," renter-friendly) leaves the **premium/craftsmanship/permanence** position entirely uncontested.
8. **Dark red/black car-shop aesthetic** alienates the home-renovation buyer; a light, warm, interior-design-adjacent visual identity will convert that audience better.
9. **No location pages, no surface-restoration content** — local-SEO landing pages and a kitchen/bath/countertop content hub can win the search terms AWS only touches with one thin page.
10. **No team/facility photos, no named certifications, no process transparency** — humanizing content (team bios, on-site process videos, certification logos) fills a trust gap they leave open.
11. **Pricing page ignores home services entirely** (car packages only) — transparent surface-restoration pricing or an instant-estimate tool would be unmatched.

---

**Overall verdict:** AWS Wrap has a competent, conversion-aware template site that is genuinely strong for its core automotive business — consistent CTAs, local-SEO discipline, WhatsApp/click-to-call, and an active blog — but its kitchen/surface offering is a thinly bolted-on vertical with recycled car imagery, no dedicated funnel, and budget-tier framing. For the surface-restoration category specifically, this is a weak competitor whose web presence signals "car shop that also wraps cabinets" rather than a trusted home-services brand. Lustre can decisively outflank them with specialist positioning, real before/after storytelling, a modern guided quote/booking flow, a concrete written guarantee, and homeowner-oriented design and content.
