# Lustre Surface Restoration — Design Research Plan

## Purpose

Use competitive design research to turn the draft design system into a stronger, evidence-informed brand and website direction.

## Research objective

Identify visual, UX, and conversion patterns from premium surface-related businesses without copying any brand directly.

## Research categories

Study websites across these categories:

1. Architectural surface film brands
2. Kitchen wrapping and cabinet wrapping companies
3. Stone restoration companies
4. Terrazzo and marble floor restoration companies
5. Luxury kitchen and bath remodelers
6. Commercial interior surface specialists
7. Premium home-service brands with strong quote funnels

## What to extract

For each target site, capture:

- URL
- Category
- Hero headline pattern
- CTA wording
- Color palette impression
- Typography impression
- Navigation structure
- Quote form fields
- Before/after gallery pattern
- Trust markers
- Warranty claims
- Proof strategy
- Residential vs commercial positioning
- Mobile UX observations
- What Lustre should emulate
- What Lustre should avoid

## Research output format

Create a table with one row per site.

Recommended columns:

| Site | Category | Hero pattern | CTA | Visual style | Proof strategy | Form UX | Trust markers | Useful pattern | Avoid |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |

## Candidate crawl targets

The final target list should be selected manually before crawling. Good target types include:

- Premium architectural film manufacturers
- Surface restoration companies with strong before/after proof
- South Florida kitchen and bath companies
- Marble and stone restoration companies
- Commercial interior wrap or architectural finish installers
- High-converting local home-service pages

## Firecrawl usage note

Firecrawl should be used to extract page structure, visible copy, metadata, and UX patterns. Do not copy proprietary copy, brand language, photography, or design assets. The goal is pattern analysis, not imitation.

## Design research questions

Answer these after crawling:

1. Which hero structure appears strongest for this category?
2. Which CTA language is most common among high-intent service pages?
3. How do premium brands balance residential and commercial audiences?
4. How do competitors show before/after proof?
5. What trust signals appear before the first CTA?
6. What form fields create minimum friction while still qualifying the lead?
7. What claims should Lustre avoid until credentials and proof exist?
8. What visual patterns would make Lustre feel premium rather than cheap?

## Deliverable

After research, update:

- `product/DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`
- `product/LANDING_PAGE_SPEC.md` if needed
- `product/WEBSITE_IMPLEMENTATION_PLAN.md` if implementation details change

## Guardrail

Do not turn design research into brand mimicry. Lustre should synthesize category patterns into its own premium surface restoration identity.
