# Lustre Surface Restoration — Pricing Model

## Pricing principle

Lustre should not compete as the cheapest installer. The pricing model should protect margin, signal professionalism, and give buyers a clear choice between value, standard, and premium finish paths.

## Pricing status

All pricing below is a launch model, not a final price list. It must be validated through real quotes, customer objections, material cost, waste rate, labor time, and warranty risk.

## Package tiers

### Investor Refresh

For STR operators, landlords, flippers, property managers, and unit-turn work.

**Positioning:** cost-efficient visual refresh for rent-ready or photo-ready surfaces.

**Estimated range:** $800–$3,000 per unit depending on scope.

Best for:

- Small counters
- Vanities
- Rental kitchens
- Basic cabinet fronts
- Repeat volume buyers

Margin guardrail:

- Do not discount below a profitable labor/material floor just to win portfolio work.
- Founding-customer discounts should be exchanged for before/after photos and reviews.

### Signature Surface Wrap

For homeowners, condo owners, and retail residential buyers.

**Positioning:** premium look without replacement cost or remodel disruption.

**Estimated range:** $1,500–$5,500 per project depending on scope.

Best for:

- Full kitchen counter refresh
- Cabinet and counter combination
- Bathroom vanity packages
- Condo kitchens
- Homeowner cosmetic upgrades

### Premium Architectural Finish

For luxury residential, commercial, hospitality, office, and high-visibility interiors.

**Positioning:** premium architectural surface transformation with commercial-grade finish selection and documentation.

**Estimated range:** $3,500–$15,000+ depending on scope.

Best for:

- Reception desks
- Retail fixtures
- Restaurant counters and bars
- Office casework
- Elevator interiors
- Premium residential surfaces

## Quote components

Every quote should account for:

- Material type
- Square footage / linear footage
- Number of edges, seams, corners, and cutouts
- Surface condition
- Required prep
- Travel zone
- Timeline urgency
- Access constraints
- Warranty risk
- Photo/video permission
- Review agreement for founding-customer discount

## Minimum job size

Recommended launch minimum:

- Standard residential minimum: $800
- Commercial minimum: $1,500
- Same-day / rush minimum: add rush premium when available

Do not allow many small jobs below minimum to consume schedule capacity unless they produce strategic proof, reviews, or referral access.

## Add-ons

Potential add-ons:

- Bathroom vanity wrap
- Backsplash film
- Cabinet pulls / hardware coordination
- Furniture or built-in wrap
- Commercial counter add-on
- Maintenance inspection
- Priority scheduling
- Extended workmanship coverage

## Discount policy

Discounts must be tied to a business objective.

Acceptable discount reasons:

- Founding-customer portfolio job
- Before/after photo permission
- Google review request after completion
- Multi-unit volume
- Referral partner pilot

Avoid:

- Discounting because the buyer negotiates aggressively
- Matching DIY material pricing
- Underpricing work with high failure risk
- Discounting premium materials into budget jobs

## Validation metrics

Track these pricing metrics for every quote:

- Quoted amount
- Material estimate
- Estimated labor hours
- Lead source
- Segment
- Accepted / rejected
- Objection reason
- Discount offered
- Actual install time
- Callback or warranty issue
- Gross margin estimate

## Pricing guardrail

If average project value falls below $800 during validation, the offer is likely attracting the wrong customer segment or the scope is being framed too narrowly. Reposition before scaling paid acquisition.
