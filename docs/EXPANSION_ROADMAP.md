# Lustre Surface Restoration — Expansion Roadmap

## Strategic decision

Lustre should launch with countertop, cabinet, vanity, and architectural surface wrapping as the first wedge, while intentionally preserving room to expand into floor, stone, tile, grout, and hard-surface restoration.

The public brand remains **Lustre Surface Restoration** because it supports both the near-term wrap offer and the later floor restoration division without requiring a rebrand.

## Why not lead with floor restoration first

Floor restoration is attractive, but it typically introduces higher operational complexity earlier:

- More equipment intensity
- More training requirements
- More dust, slurry, and site-protection complexity
- Larger liability risk on high-value stone floors
- More physically demanding jobs
- Longer path to quality control

Countertop and cabinet wrapping is the better validation wedge because it is more visually marketable, lower-capital, faster to portfolio, and easier to test with landlords, short-term rental operators, and property managers.

## Phase 1 — Surface Wrap Division

Primary focus:

- Countertop wrapping
- Cabinet wrapping
- Bathroom vanity wrapping
- Rental and Airbnb surface refreshes
- Commercial architectural surface film
- Property-manager unit-turn refreshes

Primary buyers:

- Airbnb and STR operators
- Buy-and-hold landlords
- Property managers
- Fix-and-flip investors
- Budget-conscious homeowners
- Realtors and stagers

Primary proof assets:

- Before/after kitchen photos
- Short transformation videos
- Google reviews
- Written project scopes
- Material sample library
- Referral partner relationships

## Phase 2 — Stone and Hard-Surface Restoration

Add after the surface wrap offer has validated customer acquisition and operations.

Candidate services:

- Marble polishing
- Travertine restoration
- Limestone restoration
- Terrazzo polishing
- Tile restoration
- Grout cleaning and renewal
- Stone sealing
- Shower and bathroom hard-surface renewal

Primary buyers:

- Homeowners with natural stone
- Condo owners
- Luxury rental operators
- Hotels and hospitality operators
- Property managers
- Commercial lobbies and common areas

Required buildout:

- Service-specific training
- Equipment research
- Insurance review
- Surface protection SOPs
- Dust/slurry control SOPs
- Before/after portfolio
- Maintenance-plan pricing

## Phase 3 — Floor Restoration Division

Add when Lustre can support field quality control, equipment logistics, and repeatable crew operations.

Candidate services:

- Marble floor restoration
- Terrazzo floor restoration
- Concrete polishing
- Natural stone floor polishing
- Tile and grout restoration
- Commercial floor maintenance
- Hospitality floor maintenance

Primary buyers:

- Luxury homeowners
- Condominiums and HOAs
- Hotels
- Restaurants
- Retail properties
- Offices
- Multifamily common areas

Key constraint:

Floor restoration must not be added until there is a credible training path, equipment plan, and quality-control SOP. Poor floor work can create larger reputation and liability risk than a failed wrap job.

## Phase 4 — Lustre Care Maintenance Program

Lustre Care should become the recurring-revenue layer once restoration services are active.

Potential services:

- Annual stone inspection
- Stone sealing
- Grout maintenance
- Commercial floor maintenance
- Hospitality common-area refreshes
- Priority scheduling
- Preferred customer pricing
- Surface-condition documentation

Potential plans:

### Lustre Care Residential

Annual or semiannual inspection and maintenance for homeowners with stone, tile, or restored surfaces.

### Lustre Care Rental

Maintenance and refresh support for Airbnb hosts, landlords, and property managers.

### Lustre Care Commercial

Scheduled maintenance for hotels, offices, restaurants, lobbies, and retail spaces.

## Guardrail

Do not expand the public offer faster than operational capability. The brand can promise broad surface restoration, but each service line must earn its place through training, quality control, documented SOPs, and profitable demand.
