# Lustre Surface Restoration — Operating Guardrails

## Purpose

This document prevents Lustre from scaling faster than quality, training, materials, insurance, and customer trust can support.

## Core rule

Do not let marketing outrun operational reality.

## Launch constraints

During the first validation sprint, Lustre should only sell services that can be executed or supervised at a professional standard.

Active launch services:

- Countertop wrapping
- Cabinet wrapping
- Bathroom vanity wrapping
- Small commercial architectural-film pilots
- Rental and STR refresh pilots

Future services that require separate readiness gates:

- Marble polishing
- Terrazzo restoration
- Concrete polishing
- Floor restoration
- Stone sealing
- Tile and grout restoration
- Commercial floor maintenance

## Quality guardrails

Before accepting a paid job, confirm:

- Surface is suitable for the proposed treatment
- Customer understands finish limitations
- Material has a technical data sheet
- Prep process is defined
- Edges, seams, heat zones, water zones, and cutouts are inspected
- Pre-existing damage is photographed
- Scope is written
- Warranty boundary is clear
- Customer approves finish before install

## Scope guardrails

Lustre should not perform or imply regulated trade work unless licensed or properly subcontracted.

Avoid scope creep into:

- Plumbing
- Electrical
- Structural work
- Cabinet construction
- Countertop fabrication
- Permitted remodel work

If the project requires regulated work, separate the wrap/restoration scope and refer or subcontract appropriately.

## Material guardrails

Do not use unbranded or consumer-grade material for professional paid work.

Avoid:

- Generic Amazon film
- Temu-style peel-and-stick film
- Film with no manufacturer documentation
- Film with unclear cleaning or heat guidance
- Film not suited to the surface environment

## Warranty guardrails

Separate the warranty into two categories:

1. Manufacturer or material warranty
2. Lustre workmanship warranty

Do not promise a material warranty longer or broader than the manufacturer supports. Do not offer extended workmanship terms until actual callback rates are known.

## Marketing guardrails

Allowed launch language:

- Premium surface restoration
- Countertop and cabinet wrapping
- Surface refresh without replacement
- Fast quote from photos
- Founding-customer offer
- South Florida service area

Avoid launch language:

- Certified installer unless certification is real
- Full-service floor restoration company before capability exists
- Lifetime warranty
- Heat-proof, scratch-proof, or damage-proof claims
- Guaranteed ROI or rent increase
- Any claim that cannot be supported with evidence

## Review and proof guardrails

Every early job should be used to build proof:

- Before photos
- During photos
- After photos
- Customer testimonial where available
- Google review request
- Finish/material used
- Time to complete
- Quote amount and actual time

## Expansion guardrail

A new service line should not be publicly launched until:

- Training is complete
- Insurance implications are reviewed
- Equipment is sourced
- Supplier/material standards are documented
- SOP exists
- Quality checklist exists
- At least 2 pilot jobs are documented
- Pricing model is tested

## Decision discipline

The 30-day validation scorecard controls the next move. If the scorecard is weak, do not rationalize it by saying the market needs more awareness. Fix the offer, channel, segment, or walk away.
